WHY DARK SKIES?

Experience the wonders of the universe and the beauty of the night sky.

Astrotourism is a form of tourism that focuses on experiencing the beauty of the night sky and exploring the wonders of the universe. It includes activities such as stargazing, visiting observatories, and participating in guided astronomy tours. Astrotourism has become increasingly popular in recent years as people seek to escape the bright lights of cities and immerse themselves in the peaceful darkness of the countryside.

One of the key aspects of astrotourism is the importance of dark skies. Dark skies refer to areas that are free from light pollution, allowing for clear views of the stars and other celestial objects. Light pollution is caused by the excessive use of artificial lighting, which can have negative impacts on the environment, wildlife, and human health. In addition, light pollution can obscure the night sky, making it difficult or impossible to see stars and other celestial objects.

Preserving dark skies is essential for the health of our planet and the wellbeing of its inhabitants. It allows us to appreciate the natural beauty of the night sky and encourages us to learn more about the universe. It also supports important scientific research and helps us to understand the impact of human activity on the environment.

In conclusion, astrotourism and the importance of dark skies go hand in hand. By promoting astrotourism and protecting dark skies, we can help to preserve the beauty of the night sky and promote a healthier, more sustainable planet for future generations.

My Favorite Dark-Sky Locations

Ocean

The coast is a magical place for night sky viewing with broad, sweeping views overhead right down to the sea. Full Moon rises are particularly special, with the deep-orange lunar disc slowly appearing over the water and a glittering path of light dancing on the water as it climbs higher in the sky.

Desert

The desert offers a truly enchanting experience for night sky viewing. These strange landscapes provide a surreal backdrop and expansive views. Lower humidity in desert air allows for higher clarity and visibiility. With vast open spaces and often minimal light pollution, the desert sky becomes a canvas of stars.

Learn more

Mountains

Perched high above the world, mountains provide an extraordinary vantage point for immersing oneself in the wonders of the night sky. The elevated altitude often means clearer atmospheric conditions, enhancing the visibility of celestial objects. Towering mountain summits offer an expansive panorama of the heavens and unique 360° views.
